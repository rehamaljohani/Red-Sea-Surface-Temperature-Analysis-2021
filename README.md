Red Sea Surface Temperature Analysis (2021)
Overview
This project uses Google Earth Engine (GEE) to analyze and visualize the average Sea Surface Temperature (SST) of the Red Sea during the year 2021. The data is sourced from NASA's MODIS-Aqua satellite
Key Code Steps
• Study Area: Defined a rectangle to focus specifically on the Red Sea region
• Data Source: Used the NASA/OCEANDATA/MODIS-Aqua/L3SMI dataset for high-quality oceanographic data
• Time Range: Filtered the data for the full year of 2021
• Processing: Calculated the mean (average) temperature and applied a clip to match the study area

Visual Results & Color Scale
The map uses different colors to show the Sea Surface Temperature (SST) in the Red Sea:
• 🔵 Blue (20°C and below): Shows the coolest areas, mostly found in the far North
• 🟢 Green (21°C - 25°C): Shows moderate cool waters, which are common in the Northern parts
• 🟡 Yellow (26°C - 29°C): Shows warm waters, representing the average temperature of the Red Sea
• 🔴 Red (30°C - 32°C): Shows high temperatures. The 32°C mark is a critical level for coral reef health and marine biodiversity in the South

Scientific Conclusion
The analysis reveals a significant temperature increase towards the South. This data is essential for understanding environmental stress on marine ecosystems and monitoring climate change impacts on the Red Sea's unique habitats
